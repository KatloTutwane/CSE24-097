A Vast but Flawed Space Odyssey

Bethesda's ambitious space RPG delivers exploration and depth but stumbles in execution.

Starfield is Bethesda's first new IP in decades, offering a massive universe to explore with hundreds of planets, deep customization, and classic RPG mechanics. While it excels in world-building, it suffers from some technical and pacing issues.

Gameplay and Mechanics

The game features ship customization, space combat, and faction quests. The procedural planets offer varied environments, but many feel empty. The main story is engaging, but side quests vary in quality.

Pros

  • Huge, detailed universe
  • Deep character customization
  • Engaging faction quests
  • Solid gunplay and ship combat

Cons

  • Repetitive procedural content
  • Loading screens break immersion
  • Some technical bugs

Final Verdict

Starfield is a solid RPG with incredible scope, but its flaws prevent it from reaching greatness. Fans of Bethesda's games will find much to love, but it may not convert newcomers.
